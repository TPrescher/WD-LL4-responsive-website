Costa Rica

Costa Rica is a leader in eco-tourism and sustainability. With over a quarter of its land protected as national parks and reserves, it offers lush rainforests, active volcanoes, and beautiful beaches. Visitors can explore the Monteverde Cloud Forest, spot wildlife like sloths and toucans, and enjoy eco-lodges that support conservation. Activities include zip-lining, hiking, and guided tours that teach about biodiversity and local culture. Costa Rica is also known for its commitment to renewable energy and protecting endangered species.

New Zealand

New Zealand is famous for its breathtaking scenery, from snow-capped mountains to clear blue lakes and rolling green hills. The country is dedicated to preserving its unique environment and Maori heritage. Travelers can hike the famous Milford Track, visit geothermal parks, and experience eco-friendly adventure sports. Many tours and accommodations are certified for their sustainable practices. Maldives

The Maldives is a tropical paradise made up of over 1,000 islands, known for its turquoise waters, coral reefs, and white sandy beaches. Many resorts in the Maldives focus on sustainability, using solar power, reducing plastic, and supporting marine conservation. Visitors can snorkel or dive to see vibrant coral and marine life, participate in sea turtle conservation programs, and stay at eco-resorts built with natural materials. The Maldives is committed to protecting its fragile ecosystem and promoting responsible tourism.
